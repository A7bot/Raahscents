What's the difference between perfume, cologne, and eau de toilette? ▼
The main difference is the concentration of fragrance oils:

Perfume (Eau de Parfum): 15-20% fragrance concentration. Longest lasting (6-8 hours), most luxurious, and typically the most expensive.

Eau de Toilette: 5-15% fragrance concentration. Lasts 3-5 hours, lighter and more refreshing.

Cologne (Eau de Cologne): 2-5% fragrance concentration. Lightest and shortest lasting (1-3 hours).

All are wonderful — it depends on your preference and occasion.
What are perfume oils and how are they different? ▼
Perfume oils are highly concentrated fragrance extracts (typically 30-40% fragrance concentration). They're longer lasting than traditional perfumes, require only a few drops, and are perfect for personal use. They're also great for customizing your own blends. Our oils come in different sizes and are suitable for both personal application and diffusing.
How long do fragrances last once opened? ▼
Properly stored fragrances typically last 3-5 years. To maximize shelf life:

• Keep bottles in a cool, dark place (not in direct sunlight)
• Avoid extreme temperature changes
• Keep caps tightly sealed
• Store away from humidity

The fragrance won't "go bad," but it may gradually lose potency or change slightly.

Fragrance Guidance

How do I choose the right fragrance for me? ▼
It's personal, but here are some tips:

• Consider your personality: Floral for romantic, woody for sophisticated, citrus for fresh and energetic
• Think about occasions: Light fragrances for day, heavier for evening
• Test the top notes first: The initial scent (lasts 5-15 minutes)
• Wait for the heart: The middle notes develop after 15-30 minutes
• Wear it for a few hours: The base notes emerge later (the lasting scent)

Still not sure? Message us on WhatsApp and describe your preferences. We'll recommend the perfect fragrance!
What fragrance notes mean and how to read them? ▼
Fragrances have three "layers" or notes:

Top Notes (5-15 min): First impression, usually fresh and light (citrus, herbs, spices)

Heart/Middle Notes (15 min - 1 hour): The main character, usually floral or fruity

Base Notes (1+ hours): The foundation, usually warm and lasting (woods, musk, amber)

This progression is why you should wait to judge a fragrance fully!
How much fragrance should I apply? ▼
Less is more:

• Perfume: 2-3 spritzes (on pulse points: wrists, neck, behind ears)
• Eau de Toilette: 3-4 spritzes
• Oils: Just 1-2 drops on pulse points or on clothing

Your body warmth will project the fragrance naturally. Overapplying can overwhelm others and reduce longevity. You want people to smell your fragrance when close, not from across the room!
Can fragrances be mixed or blended? ▼
Yes, absolutely! Fragrance blending is an art form. You can:

• Mix two fragrances on your skin (apply one, then the other)
• Use our perfume oils to create custom blends
• Layer fragrances with complementary scent profiles

Many people love creating their own unique signature scent. Experiment and have fun with it!
